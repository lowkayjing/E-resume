Cervical Cancer Prediction: Enhancing Early Detection Through Advanced Analytics

  • Client Taylor's University
  • Category Data Science
  • Date April, 2023

During my semester 2 at Taylor's University, I led the charge in refining our cervical cancer prediction model by meticulously preprocessing the dataset and conducting feature selection. Our primary objective was to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the predictive model, essential for early detection and intervention in cervical cancer cases. To kickstart the process, we embarked on a comprehensive data cleaning endeavor, recognizing the critical importance of handling missing values judiciously. Leveraging advanced visualization techniques, we identified attributes with missing value percentages exceeding 70% and promptly eliminated them from the dataset. Following this, we implemented strategies to replace missing values in numerical attributes with their respective medians, while categorical attributes were filled with the most frequent values.

Acknowledging the inherent imbalance in the 'Biopsy' target variable, we proactively addressed this challenge through resampling techniques utilizing the ROSE() function within the training set. Importantly, we ensured the preservation of the original configuration of the test set during this process, maintaining the integrity of our model evaluation.

In the realm of explanatory data analysis, we delved into insightful visualizations to uncover significant associations within the dataset. Through meticulously crafted bar plots and ECDF plots, we shed light on the relationships between various attributes and biopsy outcomes, offering valuable insights into the factors influencing cervical cancer diagnosis.

Our feature selection process was marked by rigorous statistical analysis and the application of random forest algorithms to pinpoint the most impactful predictors of biopsy outcomes. Following meticulous deliberation, we finalized a set of attributes deemed instrumental in accurately predicting biopsy results. These included 'Age', 'Smokes..packs.year.', 'Hormonal.Contraceptives..years.', 'Number.of.sexual.partners', 'First.sexual.intercourse', 'Dx.Cancer', 'STDs..Number.of.diagnosis', 'Dx.HPV', and 'STDs.HIV'.
